## Breakdown

_Si an ouman anta ne li tapepisu._  
**Literal:** "Two people meet at small food-shop."  
**Natural:** "Two people meet at a café."

- **Vocabulary:**
    - _Si an_: "Two" (with _si_ marking numeral usage).
    - _Ouman_: "Sentient beings" → neutral term for "people."
    - _Anta_: "Meet/gather."
    - _Ne li tapepisu_: "At small food-shop" (_ne_ = location, _li_ = small, _tapepisu_ = food-business.

_"Hai! A o Alis. E o ku?"_  
**Literal:** "Greeting! I am Alice. You are who?"  
**Natural:** "Hi! I’m Alice. What’s your name?"

- **Vocabulary:**
    - _Hai_: Universal greeting.
    - _A o_: "I am" (_a_ = I, _o_ = equative particle).
    - _E o ku?_: "You are who?" (interrogative structure).
- **Cultural Note:**
    - Omitting "name" is acceptable in Penetoki; _E o ku?_ directly asks identity.

_"Hai hai! A o Pop. Pene ku?"_  
**Literal:** "Very greeting! I am Bob. Good?"  
**Natural:** "Hello! I’m Bob. How are you?"

- **Vocabulary:**
    - _Hai hai_: Doubling for emphasis (casual/friendly tone).+
    - _Pene ku?_: "Good?" → shorthand for "Are you well?"
- **Grammar Note:**
    - _Pene ku?_ assumes context; full form could be _E o pene ku?_ ("Are you good?").

_Alis: -"A pene! E hat kohikouwai ke tehikouwai ku?"_  
**Literal:** "I good! You have coffee or tea?"  
**Natural:** "I’m great! Would you like coffee or tea?"

- **Vocabulary:**
    - _Kohikouwai_: "Coffee" (loanword: Japanese _kōhī_ + _kouwai_ "liquid").
    - _Tehikouwai_: "Tea" (loanword: Japanese _tī_ + _kouwai_).
- **Grammar Note:**
    - Double _ke_ marks a choice ("or").

_Pop: -"A ta kohikowai, mu pene. Ke maheim ro e?"_  
**Literal:** "I take coffee, much good. Homeland of you where?"  
**Natural:** "I’ll have coffee, thanks. Where are you from?"

- **Vocabulary:**
    - _Ta_: Action marker ("take/choose").
    - _Mu pene_: "Much good" → polite "thanks."
    - _Maheim ro e_: "Homeland of you" (_ro_ = possessive particle).
- **Grammar Note:**
    - _Ke maheim ro e?_ = "Where is your homeland?" (formal structure).

_Alis: -"A maheim o Kanata. E ku?"_  
**Literal:** "My homeland is Canada. You?"  
**Natural:** "I’m from Canada. And you?"

- **Vocabulary:**
    - _Maheim o Kanata_: "Homeland is Canada" (omits possessive _ro_ for brevity).
- **Cultural Note:**
    - _E ku?_: Shortened from _E maheim o ku?_ ("Your homeland is where?").

_Pop: -"A ro Intia. Pene anta e."_  
**Literal:** "Mine is India. Good meet you."  
**Natural:** "I’m from India. Nice to meet you."

- **Vocabulary:**
    - _A ro Intia_: "Mine is India" (contextual possessive).
    - _Pene anta a_: "Good meet you" → calque of English "Nice to meet you."

_Alis: -"Pene anta!"_  
**Literal:** "Good meet!"  
**Natural:** "Nice to meet you too!"


## Grammar Tips

- **Word Order**: Default C-A-C (Concept-Action-Concept), but flexible for poetry.
    - _A tape kohikouwai_ = "I drink coffee."
- **Particles**:
    - **o** = equative ("is/are").
    - **ku** = question marker (end of sentence).
    - **ta** = action marker (transforms next word to its Action role).
* **Loanwords**:
    - Follow Penetoki’s phonetic rules (e.g., coffee -> _kohi_, Canada -> *Kanata*).

# Chapter 4 - Past, Present, Future

author-comment:: Penetoki does not rely on specific grammar to express past, present or future tense, instead context and explicit time words / phrases are used.

Example:: *A hat tape timo ku.* = "I had food (in past)"

Example:: *Timo nu e hat heim.* = "(in future) You will have home."
